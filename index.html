New Cave Generation

Caves have gotten a comple overhaul, with special cave biomes like the "Lush Cave", to the stalactites and scalacmites! They are bigger, better, and more dangerous: a new mob has been added! More info on that in "Skulk Sensors and More!"

New Mountains!

After the Biome Vote from last year, mountains have finally gotten the update they deserve! New generation, snowier snow and... Goats! They jump around, can charge into you, maybe some other things which haven't been introduced yet... A nice surprsie for later!

Copper!

After a long time, there has finally come a new ore in the overworld: copper. Just like in real life, the block variant can corrode over time, turning a nice turquose color. As for usefulness: you can make lightning rods, telescopes, and maybe some other things that haven't been introduced yet...

Skulk sensors!

Skulk sensors are sensors that sense vibration, like walking and placing blocks. When they detect vibrations, they emit a redstone signal dependant on how severe the vibration is. These skulk sensor spawn only in the deepest dephts of the world, along with the Warden. The Warden is a new mini-boss mob that does... a lot of damage. But! It can only see you if you make too much noise, since it is blind. Creepy.

Axolotls!

It's been a trend lately: every Minecraft update includes an endangered species to raise awareness. In 1.17, it is the Axolotl! These adorable little fellows are able to be tamed with fish and will fight for you in the oceans. When they get low on health, they play dead until they're all healed up, so don't be worried they'll die out there. From what we know, they will spawn in lush caves and are able to be picked up with a bucket. Cute!

Archeology sites!

Lastly, we have something nobody really expected: archeology. All throughout the world, you will be able to find archeology sites where you can dust away sand and gravel in order to find hidden treasures within. Be careful though, they can break! Some of these treasures can be placed on a clay pot. Yeah Mojang, why create Minecraft lore when you can just get players to do it for you. Interesting!
